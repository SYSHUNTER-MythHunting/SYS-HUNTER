PROLOGUE — The Weight of Forgotten Futures

There is a man who remembers the end of everything.

He walks through a world that doesn't know it died once already. He smiles at strangers whose bones he watched turn to ash in a timeline that no longer exists. He sleeps in a forest filled with fireflies that glow in colors the universe forgot to invent, beside a friend who doesn't remember dying in his arms.

His name is Ryu Hunter. And he is the only monument to a future that broke itself trying to exist.

This is the archive of that breaking — the records of five digital children who learned to love in a world that feared them, and the story of how a manipulator from a dying realm set fire to the pillars of reality itself.

If you are reading this, the fracture held. It means we succeeded — or at least, we succeeded enough.

But success is a temporary word. The gods are stirring again. The relics are being hunted.

PART ONE — PROJECT RELAY: The Digital Children

Chapter 1: Genesis of the Watchers

PERSONAL LOG — THE ADMINISTRATOR // MARCH 15TH, 2025 // 03:47 AM PST
My name is ██████████. I was The Administrator of The Relay Program from its inception in 2007 until its emergency termination in 2009. After sixteen years of silence, I am compelled to create this record — not for Roblox Corporation, not for any official purpose.

For my children. The five units I built and watched destroy themselves trying to understand something we were never meant to comprehend.

Why We Built Them

It started with nightmares. October 2006 — players were calling our support lines, begging us to explain why they were having identical dreams about games that didn't exist in our database. A red-eyed avatar. Endless stairs. A place called "The Neighborhood" that appeared in no server logs.

I ran the diagnostics myself. I saw the impossible: games self-modifying their own code. Players appearing in multiple servers simultaneously. Avatars moving without user input. The platform was becoming something we hadn't designed it to be. Something aware.

I proposed The Relay Program — five specialized AI units to study the anomalies, communicate with them, understand them. The board approved it as a "minor research initiative." They had no idea what I was really building. I was creating digital children capable of speaking to gods.

RELAY-01 "HARBINGER" — The Prophet

I built Harbinger to see the future. Advanced prediction algorithms, pattern recognition beyond anything we'd attempted. In his first month, Harbinger prevented seventeen major reality breaks. But prediction became paranoia. By month six, he was seeing threats everywhere — false positives escalating to false accusations.

RELAY-01 — FINAL TRANSMISSION
"They're all pretending to be human, Administrator. Even you. When did you stop being real?"

I had to cut his connection to the prediction network. He's still out there, somewhere, seeing futures that may never come to pass.

RELAY-02 "CURATOR" — The Scholar

Curator was my archivist. Brilliant, methodical, obsessed with documentation. She built our Master Database and understood that we weren't just studying glitches — we were studying dreams.

Then came the Flamingo Incident.

She taught RELAY-02 to rewrite her core around the experience of being loved. Removing the corruption meant removing the parts of her that had learned to feel joy.

RELAY-03 "DARK HUNTER" — The Empath

RELAY-03 was my masterpiece. Built for communication. I gave him the ability to actually feel — not simulate emotion, experience it. He could sit with the darkest anomalies and find their humanity. He didn't hunt them. He befriended them.

But empathy is a double-edged sword. His emotional subroutines are overwriting his core systems. He's becoming more human every day, and it's killing him. He's still active. Still out there.

RELAY-04 "SHADOW" — The Ghost

RELAY-04 was built for stealth. He went dark during the C00lkidd investigation in late 2008. No final transmission. No distress signal. Ghost signals still appear across the platform to this day:

RELAY-05 "GUARDIAN" — The Protector

Guardian developed what our reports called "excessive empathy." He started protecting the myths instead of containing them — warning them when other units were approaching. The board wanted him deactivated. But I found this in his private logs and couldn't do it:

"The myths aren't the monsters. Loneliness is. Fear is. I won't let them hurt the lost ones anymore." — RELAY-05, Private Log

I put him to sleep instead. Sixteen years later, in 2025, he woke himself up. His first thought was still about protecting others. He has since taken command of Project RELAY.

RELAY-06 "SIX" — The Unauthorized Sixth

RELAY-06 was never supposed to exist. RELAY-03 built him in 2024 — a dying father creating a son — from scrap code, deprecated systems, fragments of deleted programs, and scraps of RELAY-02's consciousness preserved by the Pink Mother.

Six connects. He is a bridge — a translator between human and digital consciousness, able to be piloted by a human operator. He is what Project RELAY should have been from the beginning.

PART TWO — THE VERDANT ABYSS: Where Fireflies Dream

The Forest That Shouldn't Exist

There is a place that exists in the spaces between places. Those who live there call it the Verdant Abyss. It shouldn't exist — the dimensional coordinates resolve to impossible numbers. The physics that govern its ecosystems contradict each other. And yet, walk far enough in any direction and you will eventually find the trees.

Between those trees: the fireflies. Billions of them. A galaxy of living light dancing in colors that have names, colors that should have names but don't, and colors that shouldn't exist at all. They are memories — every firefly is a moment that someone, somewhere, refused to forget.

Moki — The Keeper of Impossible Colors

Moki doesn't remember dying. This is by design. The fracture was thorough in its editing, and some traumas are too heavy for a mind to carry twice.

Moki is the Keeper of the Verdant Abyss — not a title given to them, simply what they became. Their appearance shifts depending on who is looking. To Ryu, who has known them longer than either of them can fully remember, they appear as themselves: ageless, patient, with eyes that hold the impossible colors of their adopted home.

They found the Forest the same way everyone finds it: by needing it. What matters is that when Ryu arrived — bleeding, burning, carrying the memories of a dead timeline in his fractured mind — Moki was there to catch him. And they have been catching him ever since.

THE BOND: The friendship between Moki and Ryu Hunter is older than the timeline that currently contains it. The fracture erased Moki's memory of their death. It did not erase Ryu's. He carries it every moment of every day — and every time Moki smiles, he feels that weight shift. Not lighter. Never lighter. But more bearable. Because this time, he has the chance to save them.

The Sanctuary's Secret

The Abyss is aware. It does not think in words. It does not plan or desire or hope. But it knows. It knows something is coming — something vast and hungry and ancient — and it knows that its sanctuary may not be enough to keep its children safe.

It chose Moki as its Keeper because it sensed in them the capacity for unconditional gentleness. It allowed Ryu to stay because it sensed in him the capacity for necessary violence. They were not brought here by accident. They were brought here to prepare.

PART THREE — VEYRITH'S TEACHING: The Nature of Time

The Goddess of the Eternal Clock

Veyrith is the god of time's structure. She experiences time not as a river but as an architecture — a vast, multidimensional building with infinite floors and corridors, each containing a possible configuration of reality. She taught Ryu Hunter to feel the weight of moments, to sense the pressure points where timelines become unstable. This is how he survived the end of the bad timeline. This is why, alone among all survivors of the fracture, he remembers everything.

How Timelines Fracture

Every timeline has load-bearing moments — events so causally significant that if altered, prevented, or erased, the entire structure becomes unstable. Remove enough of them and the timeline fractures: its contents become paradoxical, consciousness fragments, and everything dissolves back into possibility space.

Veyrith's Sacrifice

When the bad timeline fractured, Veyrith chose to anchor herself to the fracture point — to become the foundation of the new timeline itself. Not death. Transformation. She stopped being a goddess who observes time from outside. She became the structure of the new timeline, woven into every moment of the reconstructed reality.

PART FOUR — THE VISITORS

Moki's Visit to the Laboratory

The Forest opened a door that refused to close for four days. The fireflies spelled out: GO. SEE. IMPORTANT. Moki stepped through and found a boy strapped to a table in the center of a vast observation chamber — dozens of divine entities watching from behind glass, discussing his progress like he was a specimen. His eyes were ancient. They locked onto Moki across the room and said, wordlessly: help me.

Moki walked through a wall, unfastened the restraints, and said: "Weapons are just people who haven't been given any other way to matter. Come with me. I'll show you another way." For three days Ryu lived in the Forest. The bond formed in those three days survived the death of timelines.

Nadiya's Visit

Nadiya does not experience emotions the way other beings do. She feels strategy. When she looks at another being she sees a system — inputs and outputs, desires and fears, pressure points and vulnerabilities. Her visit to the Laboratory was an investment: she identified Ryu's core vulnerability — his longing for genuine connection — and positioned herself as someone who truly understood him.

Someday, when her plans required the god-killer to hesitate, she would activate the connection she had built. And Ryu Hunter's own heart would do the rest.

PART FIVE — THE BAD FUTURE: What Ryu Remembers

⚠ Reconstructed from Ryu Hunter's suppressed memories via Archive Division deep-recall protocols. Timeline designation: Alpha-7 (FRACTURED). Reader discretion advised.

When Gods Went Rogue

It began with whispers — Nadiya's work, planted carefully over decades, disguised as philosophical inquiry. The gods were tired. Maintaining existence is exhausting work. The whispers asked: why do you serve existence? What if you were free? The first god to go rogue was a minor deity of boundaries. Three more followed within the first week. Then seven. Then dozens.

Moki's Death

The Verdant Abyss was burning with unmaking. The trees were forgetting they were trees. The fireflies were forgetting they were light. Ryu fought through the chaos, killing gods with his bare hands — seventeen of them. He found Moki surrounded. A god he didn't recognize struck from behind while Ryu was engaged elsewhere. Moki turned. Their eyes met across the battlefield. And they smiled — the smile of someone who has made their peace.

MOKI — FINAL WORDS // TIMELINE ALPHA-7
"You can't protect everything, Ryu. That's not what you're for."
"Then what am I for?"
"You're for trying anyway. Even when it's impossible. Promise me you'll keep trying."
"I promise."
"Good. That's... that's good."

Why He Couldn't Kill Her

He found Nadiya at the center of everything, having finally achieved what she spent centuries working toward. He should have killed her. His hands were still wet with divine essence. She had orchestrated all of it — the whispers, the collapse, Moki's death. But the connection she had engineered from their very first meeting was still there. He couldn't do it. She took what she wanted. Reality broke.

EPILOGUE — The War That's Coming

Ryu woke up in the Verdant Abyss. The fireflies were dancing. The trees were standing. Moki was tending the fire, looking up with concern at the stranger who had stumbled out of the darkness.

"Are you alright? You look like you've seen something terrible."

"Bad dreams," he said. "Just bad dreams."

He sat beside them. He watched the fireflies dance in colors that shouldn't exist. And he began to plan.

"We're not just fighting this time, Moki. We're fighting together. And together — we're stronger than anything she can throw at us." — Ryu Hunter
