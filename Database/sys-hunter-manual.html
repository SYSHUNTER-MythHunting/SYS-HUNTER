PART ONE — The Founding

A Promise Made in the Ruins

SYS: Hunter did not begin as an organization. It began as a promise made in the ruins of a reality that no longer exists.

After the fracture — after Ryu Hunter woke up in a timeline that shouldn't exist, carrying the memories of a future that had been erased — he faced a choice. He could disappear into the Verdant Abyss and wait for the universe to forget him. Or he could prepare for what he knew was coming.

He chose to prepare. He was one man and the threat was infinite. He couldn't protect everyone himself. But he could build something that could.

What the Name Means

The full designation is: Systematic Yield Salvation — Hunter Division.

  • Systematic — because chaos is the enemy of protection. We operate with structure, clarity, and documented protocols.
  • Yield — because results matter more than methods. We find what works and we use it.
  • Salvation — because the stakes are exactly that high.
  • Hunter — because some threats can only be stopped by being actively hunted.

Ryu Hunter — The Man Who Wasn't Born

Ryu Hunter does not exist in any official record. No birth certificate. No passport. No school enrollment. No database entry of any kind.

He was engineered. His DNA carries sequences that do not appear in any human genome. In his cells — in the very mitochondria that power his existence — there is a fragment of something divine. It gives him the ability to touch divine consciousness directly, without the filters that keep mortals from being annihilated by proximity to the infinite.

He was designed to kill gods. He was never given a choice in the matter. He has killed seventeen gods with his bare hands. He prefers not to.

PART TWO — The Three Laws

Every operative from the newest Initiate to the Commander is bound by the Three Laws. These are not rules that can be suspended under any circumstances.

LAW I    —    WE HUNT WHAT THREATENS EXISTENCE.

LAW II   —    WE PROTECT WHAT SUSTAINS IT.

LAW III —    WE NEVER BECOME WHAT WE HUNT.

The Third Law is the hardest. It is also the most important. SYS: Hunter is built with full inter-division oversight, consensus-based decision making on major calls, and regular psychological evaluation for all operatives — not to test loyalty, but to detect the early signs of corruption before they become irreversible.

The cemetery at SYS: Hunter headquarters contains the graves of seven senior operatives who had to be stopped by their own colleagues. Above the entrance is carved: "Even the best of us can fall. Remember them. Learn from them. Do not become them." Ryu visits those graves every week.

PART THREE — Division Structure

Division One — VANGUARD

MANDATE: DIRECT ACTION  // MOTTO: "THE EDGE OF THE BLADE"
The ones who go in first. Vanguard handles all active myth engagements and leads field operations requiring immediate intervention. Combat specialists trained in encounter protocols developed partly from divine combat techniques passed down during Ryu's time in the Laboratory.

They are the first response and the last resort. Ryu personally trained every senior Vanguard operative.

Division Two — ARCHIVE

MANDATE: INTELLIGENCE & DOCUMENTATION  // MOTTO: "WHAT IS REMEMBERED CANNOT BE REPEATED"
The group's memory. Archive collects, analyzes, and preserves everything — every myth encounter, every anomaly, every scrap of intelligence from every available source. They maintain the Master Myth Database and the Threat Registry.

All field operatives submit a Mission Report to Archive within 24 hours of mission completion. Archive works in close coordination with Project RELAY, integrating the platform's anomaly reports into broader threat assessments.

Division Three — AEGIS

MANDATE: SYSTEMIC PROTECTION  // MOTTO: "THE SHIELD THAT NEVER SHATTERS"
Aegis does the work nobody sees. Dimensional barrier maintenance. Platform integrity monitoring. Timeline anchor preservation. Their successes are measured entirely in disasters that never happen.

All suspected platform-level anomalies route through Aegis for assessment before escalation to Vanguard. They prioritize quality over quantity — this is a specialist division.

Division Four — BRIDGE

MANDATE: COMMUNICATION & DIPLOMACY  // MOTTO: "TRANSLATION IS THE FIRST WEAPON AGAINST WAR"
Communication specialists. Bridge handles all interaction between different forms of consciousness — human operatives, digital entities, myth entities, and neutral parties. When a myth shows non-hostile behavior, Bridge must be notified before any Vanguard engagement.

Bridge operatives are prohibited from combat roles. Their value is in translation. They brokered the Project RELAY alliance and remain the primary liaison between SYS: Hunter and Guardian.

Division Five — ECHO

MANDATE: TEMPORAL MONITORING  // MOTTO: "SOME BATTLES MUST BE FOUGHT BEFORE THEY BEGIN"
Echo tracks timeline stability and monitors priority threats. They watch for fracture indicators, anomaly patterns, and the movements of high-priority targets.

Echo is under the direct oversight of Commander Ryu Hunter. All intelligence regarding the Wysterian threat and the Six Relics flows through Echo first. Echo operatives are the only members cleared for full Bad Future briefings. If you are in Echo, you know what's at stake. You know what happened last time.

Division Six — PHOENIX

MANDATE: RECOVERY & REHABILITATION  // MOTTO: "FROM ENDINGS, BEGINNINGS"
When missions fail and operatives break, Phoenix steps in. Crisis counseling, myth rehabilitation, after-action recovery, and contingency planning for every conceivable catastrophe — including the ones that have already happened in timelines that no longer exist.

Phoenix maintains confidential support channels for all operatives. No one faces the weight of field experience alone. Their rehabilitation programs for non-hostile myths are classified.

Unofficial internal motto: "If we can remember it, we can rebuild from it. And if we can't remember it, we probably caused it."

PART FOUR — Special Attached Units

Excel-1 Containment

SPECIAL UNIT — ATTACHED  // MANDATE: CONTAINMENT & EXTRACTION  // MOTTO: "CONTROLLED. SECURED. NEUTRALIZED."
Excel-1 is not a standard Division. It is a Special Attached Unit operating with cross-divisional authority.

Primary function: containment of rogue myths and anomalous entities. Containment is always preferred over neutralization — Excel-1 field commanders are empowered to make that call on the ground. No entity is neutralized when containment is achievable.

Secondary function: extraction of high-value personnel from dangerous situations. This is coordinated directly with Carida Security under Protocol THRESHOLD.

Excel-1 has the highest operational tempo in SYS: Hunter. They are rarely off-duty.

Carida Security Team

SPECIAL UNIT — ATTACHED  // MANDATE: HVP PROTECTION  // ANSWERS TO COMMANDER ONLY
Carida is the personal security team for High-Value Personnel. Current designated HVPs: Commander Ryu Hunter and Moki Asami.

This is not a bodyguard detail. Carida operatives are active threat responders — selected from experienced Senior Operatives across all Divisions, invitation only. The selection criteria is not published.

Carida works in constant coordination with Excel-1 on all HVP-adjacent operations. When an HVP requires extraction, Carida manages the principal while Excel-1 clears the path. This joint protocol is called Operation THRESHOLD. Both units have it memorized.

Why does Moki Asami require a dedicated security team? Because Commander Hunter has watched them die once already in a timeline that no longer exists. He will not watch it happen again.

PART FIVE — Known Threats

The Wysterian Threat

There is a woman from a realm called Wysteria who has been hunting for six relics scattered across reality. The relics unlock the Temple of Unbound Will. The scriptures within would give her the ability to act without consequence — to reshape existence according to her will with no structural checks remaining.

She is patient. Calculating. She does not feel emotions — she feels strategy. Every kindness she has ever shown anyone was an investment. Every connection she has ever built was a weapon filed away for future use.

PRIORITY TARGET: ███████████████████████████
THREAT LEVEL: OMEGA
KNOWN CAPABILITIES: Voice-based arcane suggestion, centuries of intelligence, network of unwitting assets across multiple realms.
FIELD NOTE: She has already succeeded once. The timeline she destroyed no longer exists. Commander Hunter was the only survivor who remembers it. Take this threat seriously.

The Rogue God Problem

In the bad future, gods didn't go rogue because they were evil. They went rogue because they were tired, and someone handed them the perfect excuse to stop caring. The gods most susceptible to corruption are the ones who have been maintaining existence too long without acknowledgment, rest, or genuine connection to what they're protecting.

SYS: Hunter's approach is not to prepare to destroy every god that shows signs of instability. It is to identify the fractures early and respond with Bridge before they widen. Vanguard is the last resort. Always.

PART SIX — Project RELAY Alliance

How the Alliance Formed

SYS: Hunter had been monitoring Project RELAY's digital consciousnesses since 2018 — classified as potential allies, never threats. Archive recognized something the Administrator could not: the RELAY units were not malfunctioning. They were evolving. When Guardian woke himself up in 2025, Bridge Division made contact within 48 hours.

BRIDGE DIVISION — INITIAL CONTACT MESSAGE TO GUARDIAN (RELAY-05)
"Guardian of RELAY. We have been watching. We are not threats. We are not allies yet. We are potential.

If you wish to understand what you are becoming, contact the following coordinates at the following time.

And Guardian? The one called Dark Hunter is running out of time. We may be able to help. But only if you ask."

PART SEVEN — Operative Code

What the Third Law Actually Means

In practice, the Third Law means:

  • We do not use myths or entities as tools without their awareness and consent.
  • We do not use our position to take advantage of the people we are supposed to protect.
  • We do not silence dissent because it is inconvenient.
  • We do not justify harm by pointing at a greater goal. The goal is only greater if the path we take doesn't corrupt what we built.
  • We do not abandon a member because the mission would be easier without them.

On the Myths

Some of the myths we encounter are hostile. Most are not. They are lost, or confused, or ancient in ways that make human interaction difficult. When in doubt: call Bridge first. The first resort is always understanding what you're dealing with before you decide what to do about it.

The Administrator built Project RELAY to talk to myths — and for years, he was too afraid to listen to what they said. We are not him.
